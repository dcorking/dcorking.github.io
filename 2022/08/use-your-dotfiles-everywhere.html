Use your dotfiles everywhere

2022-08-28 :: Linux, macOS, git

By: David

A few years ago I saw an example of a git repository used to synchronize dot files across every Linux machine that the person worked on. So I made my own dotfiles repo,

and I also used it to share some other handy scripts I had collected. I soon extended it to my OS X, then macOS, setups.

I thoroughly recommend the convenience of having all your tools configured the same way, your way, on every development machine you use.

But I read recently that several more tools have been written, adding even more automation to sharing your Unix home directory preferences. I am tempted to try one of five new options in the comparison table shared by the chezmoi project
